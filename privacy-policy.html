PrivacyLens Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 18, 2026

PrivacyLens is an offline Android privacy review tool. It analyzes installed app metadata and Android permission states locally on your device.

What PrivacyLens Accesses

PrivacyLens may read installed app metadata, including:

  • Installed app names
  • Package names
  • App icons
  • Requested permissions
  • Android-reported permission grant states
  • App install and update dates

This information is used only to show permission explanations, privacy review labels, PDF reports, and risk scores inside the app.

What PrivacyLens Does Not Access

PrivacyLens does not read:

  • Messages or chats
  • Photos, videos, or files
  • Passwords
  • Private app databases
  • Exact network traffic
  • Exact moments when another app uses a permission

Data Collection and Sharing

PrivacyLens does not upload personal data, app lists, scan results, reports, or settings to any server.

PrivacyLens does not use accounts, login, analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, tracking SDKs, or backend APIs by default.

Local Data

PrivacyLens stores some choices locally on your device, such as ignored warnings, trusted app choices, manual app category overrides, and onboarding completion. You can reset these from the in-app Settings screen.

PDF reports are generated locally on your device. Sharing a report happens only when you choose Android's share option.

Children's Privacy

PrivacyLens is a general privacy utility and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The app does not collect or upload personal data.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new updated date.

Contact

For support or privacy questions, contact lensprivacy@gmail.com.

PrivacyLens works offline. No cloud. No tracking. No account required.
